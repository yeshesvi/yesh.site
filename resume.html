Tips by text - Anonymous tips to the Police

Desktop Software Design and User Experience Research

Me along with two other University of Michigan graduate students have worked alongside the Jackson Police Department to design and create a software program for the police to receive anonymous texts. With the help of this software, residents of Jackson are able to send anonymous tips from their mobile phones (both feature phones and smartphones) without revealing their identity to the police. The police receive the entirety of the tip, but the phone number is stripped from the tip and encrypted by the program. The project was developed with intense consideration to reduction of operating, training and maintenance costs.

<

> The system can be setup with a minimal 200$ desktop and a 100$ per month cost. Software is also accessible via web and designed to look closely like an email client that the police are trained to use already. This allows police to disperse justice better while tip callers don't risk being labeled in their neighborhoods.
